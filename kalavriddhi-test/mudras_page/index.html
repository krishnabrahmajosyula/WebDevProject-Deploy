Mudras

Mudras are symbolic hand gestures that are important in yoga, meditation, and Indian classical dance because they can:
1. Balance the body
2.Improve mental activity
3.Relieve stress

Pataka

A hand gesture symbolizing a flag or stop.

Tripataka

A hand gesture symbolizing a crown or tree.

Simhamukha

A hand gesture symbolizing representing division, balance, or unity in duality.

Kartarimukha

A hand gesture symbolizing scissors or separation.

Arala

A hand gesture symbolizing a bent hand or wind.

Shukatunda

A hand gesture symbolizing a parrot's beak.

Chandrakala

A hand gesture symbolizing the moon's crescent and is often used to convey beauty, love, and tranquility.

Ardhachandra

A hand gesture representing the half-moon, often associated with beauty and grace.

Mrigashirsha

A hand gesture that represents the head of a deer, symbolizing gentleness and grace.

Katakamukha

A hand gesture resembling a bracelet and symbolizing the idea of a closed circle, representing completeness and unity.

Sarpashirsha

A hand gesture signifing a snake's head and embodies themes of transformation and renewal.

Suchi

A hand gesture symbolizing purity and cleanliness.
